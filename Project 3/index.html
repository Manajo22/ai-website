About Work

Red Bite

Medium: Powder-coated steel, weather-treated glass, high-lumen LED core
Installation Site: Bayou Walkway, Everglades Edge
Year: 2023

Red Bite is a public lighting sculpture rooted in the mythos and menace of the Everglades’ lesser-remembered residents—the piranhas that once swam its deeper waters. Installed along a secluded bayou trail, this street lamp leans into the visceral edge of nature, contrasting Moi Faux’s typically serene inspirations with something more primal and alert. The form evokes the arched motion of a striking fish, with jagged steel elements resembling dorsal fins and a tooth-like cutout design wrapping the base. Housed within the mouth-like upper structure, a high-lumen LED source casts a stark downward glow—cutting through night fog and low canopies like the glint of a predator's eye beneath water. Unlike his more meditative indoor pieces, Red Bite confronts viewers. It transforms a utilitarian object into a symbol of ecological tension, fear, and misunderstood beauty. The durable materials—industrial steel and treated glass—were chosen not only for longevity in humid bayou conditions, but also to underscore the raw and armored nature of its subject. At night, the lamp does more than light the path—it tells a quiet story of caution and respect for the wild. Red Bite is a reminder: not all beauty in nature is soft, and not all danger is ugly.
