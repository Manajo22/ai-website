About Work

Medusan Stillness

Medium: Molded translucent plastic, embedded filament lighting, flexible silicone tendrils
Dimensions: Approx. 22” x 14”
Year: 2025
Inspiration: Coastal waters, Northern Australia

Medusan Stillness is an homage to the quiet, almost alien grace of the jellyfish—creatures that embody contradiction: delicate yet dangerous, translucent yet opaque in mystery. Conceived during Moi Faux’s travels along Australia’s northern coasts, the piece was born from an encounter not of fear, but reverence—a recognition of the jellyfish as a floating vessel of tension and elegance. The sculptural lamp is molded from translucent plastic, shaped into a soft, domed body that pulses with a low, bioluminescent-like glow from its embedded filament. Below, silicone tendrils dangle and shimmer with even the slightest air movement, mimicking the undulating drift of a jellyfish in water. The overall form is purposefully spare—an exercise in restraint to match the animal's austere beauty. For Moi Faux, this work represents a turning inward: a meditation on fragility, presence, and the quiet warnings embedded in the natural world. The jellyfish becomes a vessel not just of light, but of awareness—something to behold, but never fully grasp. Medusan Stillness invites viewers to linger in the in-between space it occupies: between sculpture and organism, presence and illusion, light and shadow.
