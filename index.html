About

Moi Faux is an artist, designer, and industrial innovator whose work bridges the organic elegance of the natural world with contemporary functional design. Raised in the heart of the Everglades, Florida, his creative identity is deeply rooted in the rhythms and subtleties of wetland ecosystems. Drawing inspiration from biomimicry—the design and production of materials modeled on biological entities—Moi transforms the often-overlooked forms of nature into imaginative, tactile experiences. Specializing in lamp making, Moi Faux crafts lighting pieces that blur the line between artifact and organism. His designs, often mimicking the structure of native Everglades flora or the interplay of light on swamp waters, are celebrated for their warmth, inventiveness, and environmental sensitivity. With a thoughtful, introspective presence and a quiet confidence, Moi brings a contemplative touch to each project—turning utilitarian objects into meditative art forms. His signature aesthetic combines minimalism with a reverence for natural textures and patterns, resulting in works that feel simultaneously modern and ancient. Whether he's sketching in his sun-drenched studio or foraging for design inspiration in the wild, Moi Faux remains committed to honoring the source of his creativity: nature itself.
