About Work

Anisoptera I

Medium: Stained glass, industrial wireframe, internal LED lighting
Dimensions: Approx. 48” x 60”
Year: 2025

Anisoptera I emerges from Moi Faux’s childhood memories of the Everglades, where dragonflies darted across still waters with quiet precision. This suspended lighting sculpture captures both the delicate motion and anatomical strength of its natural muse. Hand-formed stained glass wings—each one unique in tone and contour—are traced with minimalist wire veins that echo the biological geometry of dragonfly wings. Suspended by tensioned industrial metal wire, the form floats with sculptural lightness, while its illuminated thorax radiates a soft, amber warmth that evokes the golden hour light of marshland evenings. As both a functional light and a contemplative object, the piece invites viewers to pause and consider the beauty of balance: fragility and structure, nature and design, nostalgia and innovation. Faux's use of biomimicry is more than aesthetic—it is a gesture of reverence. The dragonfly, a symbol of transformation and adaptability, becomes a medium through which light takes flight.
