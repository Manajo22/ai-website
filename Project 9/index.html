About Work

Tembo

Medium:Teak wood, steam-bent laminate
Type:Suspended lamp sculpture

Born from a journey through Tanzania’s expansive safari reserves, Tembo channels the gentle gravity of the African elephant into a lighting form that is both reverent and sculptural. Using teak wood and traditional steam-bending techniques, Moi Faux shaped the contours of this piece to echo the curve of an elephant’s trunk and the drape of its ears—expressing both strength and grace in equal measure. The lamp’s warm tones and organic silhouette evoke a creature mid-motion, frozen in a moment of stillness. More than a lighting object, Tembo becomes a quiet homage to the intelligence and presence of one of nature’s most majestic beings. Subtle shadows ripple across its surface when illuminated, recalling the sun-filtered terrain of the savanna and the hushed reverence of the wild. A favorite of the artist’s, Tembo stands as a contemplative embodiment of nature’s form, memory, and movement—transformed through human hands, but never separated from its source.
