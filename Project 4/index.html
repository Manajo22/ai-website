About Work

Nestlight

Medium: Hand-woven cane, organic resin finish, suspended LED filament
Commissioned for: Private Residence, Coral Gables, FL
Year: 2024

Crafted as a symbol of domestic serenity, Nestlight draws its form and spirit from the delicate, intentional architecture of birds that build homes—not for defense, but for care. Woven by hand from locally sourced cane, the piece evokes the cradle-like curves of a nest suspended safely above ground, holding light like warmth held within a family. Commissioned for a private residence by a client who spoke of home as both haven and heritage, Moi Faux translated these values into physical design. The cane—selected for its natural pliability and warm hue—was shaped into a form that feels alive: slightly asymmetrical, responsive, like something created over time rather than produced. An internal LED filament glows through the weave, casting a pattern of soft shadows and golden ripples across the ceiling, much like filtered sunlight through leaves or feathers. The result is a piece that doesn’t dominate a room but quietly blesses it—inviting stillness, safety, and shared space. Nestlight is more than an object; it’s a tribute to the unspoken rituals of family, the artistry of nature’s builders, and the light we carry in places we call home.
