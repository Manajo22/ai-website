About Work

Caudal Glow

Medium: Glazed ceramic, frosted glass, integrated LED
Dimensions: Approx. 10” x 6”
Commissioned by: Gourmet Foods Restaurant
Year: 2024

Designed as a bespoke lighting piece for the coastal-themed interiors of Gourmet Foods, Caudal Glow takes its form from the flowing tail fin of a goldfish—a creature both ornamental and instinctual, domestic yet deeply tied to the rhythms of water. Crafted from hand-shaped ceramic and capped with frosted glass, the fixture emits a diffused, ambient light that mimics the soft refracted glow seen underwater. The vibrant orange base transitions into a gentle opacity, echoing the gradient found in natural goldfish coloration. The result is a wall-mounted light that feels alive—poised mid-motion, like a fin pushing gently through a current. Moi Faux approached this project with his signature biomimetic ethos, treating the goldfish not just as a symbol, but as a study in elegance and adaptability. The choice of materials reflects both functionality and tactility, producing a subtle but immersive atmosphere within the dining space. Whether installed singly or in a rhythmic series across the restaurant’s interior, Caudal Glow acts as both utility and gesture—a nod to nature’s sculptural logic, brought to life through light.
