About Work

Another Light

Medium: Mixed media, linen and steel armature
Pendant lamp

In the quiet heat of Florida’s wetlands, grasshoppers are both companions and curiosities. Another Light reimagines these often-overlooked creatures—so frequently stepped around or swatted away—as airy, delicate beacons of wonder. Built from a skeletal steel armature and gossamer linen wings, the lamp’s form hovers between anatomical study and dreamlike apparition. Its internal glow gives the insect a new narrative: no longer just a critter of the underbrush, but a carrier of light, balance, and unlikely beauty. Moi Faux captures not just the shape of the grasshopper, but the quiet surprise of encountering one at rest. In giving the insect scale and illumination, this piece invites us to reconsider what we dismiss as ordinary—and instead, see it anew.
